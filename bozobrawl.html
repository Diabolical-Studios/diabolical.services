BOZO BRAWL GDD

1. Introduction/Summary

Title: Bozo Brawl

Developed by: Diabolical Studios

Genre: Party Game

Overview

Welcome to the chaotic, hilarious, and endlessly entertaining world of Bozo Brawl! Bozo Brawl is a dynamic arena party game where you and your friends can engage in a variety of mini-games, each with its own unique objectives and gameplay mechanics. The goal? To be the ultimate winner, of course!

Gameplay Loop

  1. Players join a lobby with their friends.
  1. The group selects a game mode from a diverse list of options.
  1. The chosen mini-game begins, and players compete to achieve the specific goals of that mode.
  1. Once the mini-game concludes, players return to the lobby.
  1. The group selects a new game mode, and the cycle repeats.

Objective

The objective of Bozo Brawl is simple: have fun and aim to win! Each game mode offers different goals, ranging from collecting the most items to being the last one standing. The game is designed to keep players engaged, offering a fresh experience every time they return to the lobby to select a new game mode.

Target Audience

Bozo Brawl is perfect for players who love social experiences, quick matches, and a healthy dose of competition and little kids who want to have a fun time with their mobile phones/tablets in their free time. Whether you're hosting a game night or just looking for a quick gaming session with friends, Bozo Brawl offers endless hours of laughter and excitement.

2. Game Mechanics

Player Control Mechanics

In Bozo Brawl, players have access to five core control mechanics that govern their interactions within the game world:

  1. Run: Players can move quickly across the game environment.
  1. Jump: Allows players to navigate vertical spaces and avoid obstacles.
  1. Punch: A basic attack to engage opponents and interact with objects.
  1. Grab: Players can grab onto objects or other players.
  1. Throw: After grabbing, players have the option to throw objects or opponents.

These mechanics serve as the foundation for gameplay and are consistent across all game modes, providing a unified player experience.

Dynamic Events

To spice things up and keep players on their toes, most levels feature dynamic events that impact gameplay:

  1. Helicopter Wind: In certain levels, a helicopter flies by, creating strong winds that push players around. The objective here is to maintain your footing and avoid being blown off a building.
  1. Sunburn: In sun-exposed levels, players risk getting burned if they stay out of water for too long. As the sun sets and night falls, the entire level becomes a safe zone for brawling.

These dynamic events add an extra layer of strategy and excitement, forcing players to adapt their tactics on the fly. We’ll also be adding lots of new maps so that means there will be even more dynamic events to come.

Interactions and Combat

Grab and Throw Objects

Players can interact with various objects in the game environment. Grabbing and throwing objects can serve multiple purposes, such as creating obstacles or using them as improvised weapons.

Grabbing Players

Players can also grab onto other players, either to hinder their movement or to throw them into hazards.

Punch and Stun

A well-timed punch can stun an opponent, making them vulnerable to further attacks or environmental hazards.

3. Gameplay

Lobby Experience

Before diving into the competitive chaos, players find themselves in a free-roam lobby. Here, they can practice their basic moves—running, jumping, punching, grabbing, and throwing—while interacting with other players. Think of it as a sandbox where you can mess around and get a feel for the controls.

Starting a Game

To kick off the action, players enter a designated door in the lobby. This triggers the game mode selection screen, where the group chooses which mini-game they'd like to play first.

Core Gameplay

Once a game mode is selected, players are transported to the corresponding level. Regardless of the specific objectives of each mini-game, the core mechanics (punch, run, grab, throw, jump) remain consistent. Players use these actions to mess with each other and achieve the goals of the chosen game mode.

Environmental Interactions

Many levels feature dynamic events or environmental assets that players can use to their advantage. Whether it's dodging helicopter winds or fighting in a water pit to avoid sunburn, these elements add an extra layer of strategy and unpredictability.

Redemption Mini-Game

After each round, a special mini-game level is triggered. The player who scored the lowest in the previous game gets a "redemption chance" to earn extra points. However, they'll have to overcome a level where every other player is pitted against them. It's a high-risk, high-reward scenario that keeps everyone engaged, regardless of their performance in the previous round.

Game Loop Summary

  1. Players enter the lobby and mess around.
  1. The group chooses a game mode.
  1. Players compete in the selected mini-game.
  1. Dynamic events and environmental assets come into play.
  1. The game concludes, and points are tallied.
  1. A redemption mini-game is triggered for the lowest-scoring player.
  1. Players return to the lobby to select a new game mode.
  1. Repeat steps 3-7 until the game session ends.

5. Art Style

General Aesthetic

Bozo Brawl boasts a low-poly, stylized art direction. The overall look and feel is "rubbery," giving the game a playful, whimsical atmosphere that complements its party-game nature.

Character Design

The main characters are designed to be cute and goofy, resembling fast-moving pears with exaggerated, elongated arms, fingers, and legs. Their quirky appearance sets the tone for the lighthearted, chaotic gameplay.

Skins

Players have the option to collect or purchase skins to customize their characters. These skins range from simple color changes to more elaborate designs, allowing players to make their characters even cuter or goofier according to their personal taste.

Level Design

Oasis Island

This map transports players to a tropical paradise, complete with palm trees, sandy beaches. The low-poly aesthetic is maintained, but the environment is rich in color and detail.

Cyberpunk City

In stark contrast, the Cyberpunk City map offers a darker, more futuristic setting. Neon lights, towering skyscrapers, and digital billboards create a visually stimulating playground.

Dynamic Elements

Both the characters and the environments are designed to interact seamlessly with the game's dynamic events. Whether it's the rubbery characters struggling against a helicopter's wind or scrambling to stay inside the water in the Oasis Island map, the art style is integral to the gameplay experience.

6. Technology

Engine

Bozo Brawl is developed using the Unity engine, chosen for its flexibility, robust community support, and ease of use for both 2D and 3D game development.

Graphics and Visual Effects

Volume and Post-Processing

To enhance the game's visual appeal, we're utilizing Unity's built-in volume and post-processing features. These tools allow us to fine-tune the game's lighting, color grading, and other visual elements to match our desired aesthetic.

Beautify

In addition to Unity's native features, we're also using Beautify, a third-party asset, to further polish the game's visuals. This helps us achieve a more refined look, enhancing the game's overall visual quality.

Special Character Behaviors

One of the more complex aspects of development has been implementing the "jiggle and bounce" behaviors for the main characters. This required specialized physics calculations and fine-tuning to achieve the desired rubbery, playful feel.

7. UI/UX

General Approach

Bozo Brawl takes a unique approach to UI by integrating menu options directly into the game environment. The main menu is essentially the lobby, where players can interact with various objects to access settings, start games, and more.

In-Game Interactions for Menu Options

Volume Control

To adjust the game's volume, players can grab and slide a lever in the lobby. This physical interaction replaces the traditional volume slider found in most game menus.

Mute Option

If players wish to mute the game, they can do so by punching a designated button in the environment. This provides a quick and intuitive way to control audio settings.

Starting a Game

To initiate a game session, players must open and enter a specific door in the lobby. This action serves as the trigger to start the game and move on to the game mode selection screen.

Design Philosophy

The UI is designed to be as immersive and intuitive as possible, allowing players to control game settings without breaking the flow of gameplay. The simple, stylized aesthetic of the UI elements complements the game's overall art style.

8. Audio

Music

The musical score of Bozo Brawl is designed to complement the level's thematic elements while maintaining a consistent, overarching style. The music serves as funny background tunes that add tension and excitement to the gameplay.

Level-Specific Themes

Each level features its own unique musical theme that aligns with its design and atmosphere. For example, the Oasis Island map might feature tropical, upbeat tunes, while the Cyberpunk City map could have a more electronic, futuristic soundtrack.

Sound Effects

Character Sounds

The sound effects for the player characters are intentionally cute and endearing. The characters sound like they're having a hard time, which adds a layer of humor and personality to the game.

Particle Effects

We'll be making extensive use of Unity's particle system for various in-game effects. Each particle effect will be accompanied by its own sound effect to enhance the sensory experience.

Design Philosophy

The audio in Bozo Brawl is crafted to enhance the player's emotional and sensory experience. From the tension-building background music to the cute, humorous character sounds, the audio serves to complement the game's whimsical, competitive atmosphere.
